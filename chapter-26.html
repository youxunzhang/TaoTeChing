Chapter 26

The Heavy and Light

Original Chinese

重為輕根，靜為躁君。
是以聖人終日行不離輜重。
雖有榮觀，燕處超然。
奈何萬乘之主，而以身輕天下？
輕則失根，躁則失君。

English Translation

The heavy is the root of the light.
The still is the master of the restless.
Therefore the sage, traveling all day, does not lose sight of his baggage.
Though there are beautiful things to be seen, he remains tranquil and unattached.
Why should the lord of a thousand chariots behave lightly in public?
To be light is to lose one's root.
To be restless is to lose one's self-control.
