Chapter 72

When People Do Not Fear Power

Original Chinese

民不畏威，則大威至。
無狎其所居，無厭其所生。
夫唯不厭，是以不厭。
是以聖人自知不自見；
自愛不自貴。
故去彼取此。

English Translation

When people do not fear your power,
then great power has arrived.
Do not interfere with their dwellings;
do not oppress their livelihood.
Only by not oppressing them
will you not be oppressed.
Therefore the sage knows himself
but does not display himself;
loves himself but does not exalt himself.
Thus he rejects that and chooses this.

True Authority

This chapter reveals the paradox of power and authority. When people are no longer afraid of artificial power, true power emerges. Real authority comes not from oppression or display, but from self-knowledge and genuine care for others' welfare. The sage leads by example rather than force.
