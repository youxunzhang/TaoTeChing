Chapter 48

Forgetting Knowledge

Original Chinese

為學日益，為道日損。
損之又損，以至於無為。
無為而無不為。
取天下常以無事，
及其有事，不足以取天下。

English Translation

In pursuit of learning, every day something is added.
In pursuit of the Tao, every day something is dropped.
Less and less is done
until non-action is achieved.
When nothing is done, nothing is left undone.
The world is ruled by letting things take their course.
It cannot be ruled by interfering.

简体中文

Two Paths

This chapter reveals the fundamental difference between conventional learning and spiritual wisdom. Academic learning accumulates information, facts, and theories, constantly adding to our mental burden. Following the Tao, however, involves stripping away illusions, attachments, and unnecessary complexities until we reach a state of natural simplicity. Through non-action (wu wei), we achieve everything by doing nothing that goes against the natural flow of life.
