Chapter 49

The Sage's Heart

Original Chinese

聖人常無心，以百姓心為心。
善者，吾善之；不善者，吾亦善之；
德善。信者，吾信之；
不信者，吾亦信之；德信。
聖人在天下，歙歙焉，
為天下渾其心，
百姓皆注其耳目，
聖人皆孩之。

English Translation

The sage has no fixed mind of his own;
he makes the mind of the people his mind.
To those who are good I am good,
and to those who are not good I am also good.
This is true goodness.
To those who are sincere I am sincere,
and to those who are not sincere I am also sincere.
This is true sincerity.
The sage lives in the world peacefully
and harmonizes his heart with the world.
People fix their eyes and ears on him,
and the sage treats them all as children.

Universal Compassion

This beautiful chapter describes the sage's unconditional love and acceptance. Unlike ordinary people who judge and discriminate, the sage responds to everyone with equal kindness and trust. This doesn't mean being naive, but rather embodying a higher form of goodness that can transform even difficult people through its consistency. The sage treats all people as precious children, seeing their essential nature beyond their current behavior.
