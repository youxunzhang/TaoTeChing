Chapter 76

Flexibility and Strength

Original Chinese

人之生也柔弱，其死也堅強。
草木之生也柔脆，其死也枯槁。
故堅強者死之徒，柔弱者生之徒。
是以兵強則滅，木強則折。
強大處下，柔弱處上。

English Translation

When people are born, they are soft and weak;
when they die, they are stiff and strong.
When plants are born, they are tender and fragile;
when they die, they are withered and hard.
Therefore the stiff and strong are companions of death;
the soft and weak are companions of life.
Thus if an army is strong, it will not win;
if a tree is strong, it will break.
The strong and great take the lower position;
the soft and weak take the higher position.

The Power of Softness

This chapter presents one of Taoism's most fundamental insights: the strength of flexibility over rigidity. Life is characterized by softness and adaptability, while death brings stiffness and brittleness. The wise person cultivates flexibility and yielding, understanding that these qualities lead to longevity and success.
