Chapter 79

The Covenant

Original Chinese

和大怨，必有餘怨；
報怨以德，安可以為善？
是以聖人執左契，
而不責於人。
有德司契，無德司徹。
天道無親，常與善人。

English Translation

When great resentments are reconciled,
some resentment inevitably remains.
How can this be considered good?
Therefore the sage holds the left half of the contract
but does not make demands on others.
Those with virtue attend to their part of the contract;
those without virtue attend to collecting debts.
The Way of Heaven shows no favoritism,
yet always supports good people.

The Limits of Reconciliation

This chapter acknowledges the difficulty of complete reconciliation. Even when great conflicts are resolved, some lingering resentment often remains. The sage focuses on fulfilling their own obligations rather than demanding that others meet theirs. The metaphor of holding the contract but not demanding payment illustrates true virtue.
