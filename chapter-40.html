Chapter 40

Chinese Original

反者道之动，弱者道之用。

天下万物生于有，有生于无。

English Translation

Returning is the movement of the Tao; yielding is the function of the Tao.

All things in the world are born from being; being is born from non-being.

Commentary

This concise chapter captures two fundamental principles of Taoist philosophy. First, the Tao operates through return and yielding—what appears to be retreat or weakness is actually the source of strength and renewal. Second, the relationship between being and non-being reveals that existence emerges from emptiness, form from formlessness. This paradox suggests that what we consider "nothing" is actually the source of everything, and true power comes from understanding and working with apparent weakness.
