Chapter 33

Chinese Original

知人者智，自知者明。

胜人者有力，自胜者强。

知足者富，强行者有志。

不失其所者久，死而不亡者寿。

English Translation

Knowing others is wisdom; knowing yourself is enlightenment.

Conquering others requires force; conquering yourself requires strength.

Those who know contentment are rich; those who persevere have will.

Those who do not lose their place endure; those who die but are not forgotten achieve longevity.

Commentary

This chapter presents profound contrasts between external and internal mastery. Laozi distinguishes between worldly intelligence and true enlightenment, between physical force and inner strength. The highest achievement is not conquering others but mastering oneself. True wealth comes from contentment rather than accumulation, and genuine longevity is achieved through leaving a lasting impact rather than merely extending physical life.
