Chapter 62

The Tao

Original Chinese

道者萬物之奧。
善人之寶，不善人之所保。
美言可以市尊，美行可以加人。
人之不善，何棄之有？
故立天子，置三公，
雖有拱璧以先駟馬，
不如坐進此道。
古之所以貴此道者何？
不曰：求以得，有罪以免邪？
故為天下貴。

English Translation

The Tao is the secret treasure of all things.
It is the good person's treasure
and the protection of those who are not good.
Beautiful words can buy honor;
good deeds can gain respect.
If people are not good, why abandon them?
Therefore, when the emperor is enthroned
and the three ministers are installed,
rather than presenting jade discs
followed by teams of horses,
it is better to remain still and offer this Tao.
Why did the ancients treasure this Tao?
Is it not because through it
those who seek will find,
and those with guilt will be forgiven?
Therefore it is treasured by all under heaven.

Universal Treasure

This chapter presents the Tao as a universal treasure that benefits all people, regardless of their moral standing. Unlike material gifts or political honors, the Tao offers something far more valuable - transformation and redemption. It provides protection and guidance to all who seek it, making it the ultimate treasure.
