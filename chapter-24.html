Chapter 24

Bitter Food

Original Chinese

企者不立；
跨者不行；
自見者不明；
自是者不彰；
自伐者無功；
自矜者不長。
其在道也，曰：餘食贅行。
物或惡之，故有道者不處。

English Translation

He who stands on tiptoe is not steady.
He who strides forward does not go far.
He who displays himself does not shine.
He who asserts his own views is not respected.
He who boasts of himself is not given credit.
He who is arrogant is not a good leader.
In the Tao, these are called "excess food and burdensome actions."
Things of this sort are repugnant to all creatures.
Therefore, he who would follow the Tao does not do such things.
