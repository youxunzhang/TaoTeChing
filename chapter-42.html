Chapter 42

Chinese Original

道生一，一生二，二生三，三生万物。

万物负阴而抱阳，冲气以为和。

人之所恶，唯孤、寡、不谷，而王公以为称。

故物或损之而益，或益之而损。

人之所教，我亦教之。强梁者不得其死，吾将以为教父。

English Translation

The Tao gives birth to One, One gives birth to Two, Two gives birth to Three, Three gives birth to all things.

All things carry yin and embrace yang, achieving harmony through the interaction of these forces.

What people hate are the terms "orphaned," "widowed," and "worthless," yet rulers use these as their titles.

Therefore, things are sometimes diminished by being increased, and increased by being diminished.

What others teach, I also teach: "The violent and aggressive do not die natural deaths." I will make this the foundation of my teaching.

Commentary

This chapter presents Laozi's cosmology—how the universe unfolds from the Tao through progressive stages of manifestation. The interplay of yin and yang creates harmony in all things. The paradox of rulers adopting humble titles shows how true strength comes from acknowledging weakness. The principle that loss can become gain and gain can become loss reflects the dynamic nature of existence, while the warning about violence emphasizes that aggressive behavior ultimately leads to destruction.
