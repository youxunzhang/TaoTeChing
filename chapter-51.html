Chapter 51

The Virtue of the Tao

Original Chinese

道生之，德畜之，
物形之，勢成之。
是以萬物莫不尊道而貴德。
道之尊，德之貴，
夫莫之命而常自然。
故道生之，德畜之；
長之育之；成之熟之；
養之覆之。
生而不有，為而不恃，
長而不宰。是謂玄德。

English Translation

The Tao gives birth to all things,
and the virtue of the Tao nourishes them.
Matter shapes them,
and environment perfects them.
Therefore all things honor the Tao
and value its virtue.
The honor of the Tao
and the value of its virtue
are not decreed by any authority
but arise naturally.
The Tao gives birth to all things,
nourishes them, develops them,
cares for them, comforts them,
protects them, takes them back to itself,
creating without possessing,
acting without expecting,
guiding without forcing.
This is called the mysterious virtue.

Divine Virtue

This chapter reveals the cosmic role of the Tao as the source and sustainer of all existence. The Tao creates without claiming ownership, nurtures without expecting return, and guides without controlling. This mysterious virtue (xuan de) represents the highest form of power— one that operates through love and wisdom rather than force and domination.
