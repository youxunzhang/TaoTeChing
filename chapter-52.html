Chapter 52

Return to the Origin

Original Chinese

天下有始，以為天下母。
既得其母，以知其子，
既知其子，復守其母，
沒身不殆。
塞其兌，閉其門，
終身不勤。
開其兌，濟其事，
終身不救。
見小曰明，守柔曰強。
用其光，復歸其明，
無遺身殃；是為習常。

English Translation

The universe has a beginning.
Call it the mother of the universe.
Once you have the mother,
you can know the children.
Once you know the children,
return and abide in the mother.
Your whole life will be preserved from harm.
Block the openings,
shut the doors,
and all your life you will not toil.
Unblock the openings,
multiply your activities,
and all your life you will remain beyond help.
Seeing the small is clarity.
Keeping to the soft is strength.
Use your light and return to clarity.
Leave nothing to harm your person.
This is practicing the constant.

The Constant Practice

This chapter teaches about returning to the source. Once we understand the multiplicity of manifestations (the children), we must return to the original unity (the mother). By closing off excessive desires and activities, we preserve our essential energy. True strength comes from maintaining softness, and true wisdom from seeing the small details that others miss.
