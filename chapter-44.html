Chapter 44

Fame or Life

Original Chinese

名与身孰亲？
身与货孰多？
得与亡孰病？
甚爱必大费；
多藏必厚亡。
故知足不辱，
知止不殆，
可以长久。

English Translation

Fame or life: which is more dear?
Life or wealth: which is worth more?
Gain or loss: which is more painful?
He who loves most spends most;
He who hoards most loses most.
The contented person is never disappointed;
He who knows when to stop does not find himself in trouble;
He will stay forever safe.

Reflection

This chapter poses fundamental questions about what we truly value. It challenges us to consider whether external recognition, material wealth, or our essential well-being is most important. The wisdom teaches that excessive attachment to fame or possessions leads to suffering, while contentment and knowing our limits lead to lasting peace and security.
