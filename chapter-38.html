Chapter 38

Chinese Original

上德不德，是以有德；

下德不失德，是以无德。

上德无为而无以为；

下德无为而有以为。

上仁为之而无以为；

上义为之而有以为。

上礼为之而莫之应，则攘臂而扔之。

故失道而后德，失德而后仁，失仁而后义，失义而后礼。

夫礼者，忠信之薄，而乱之首。

English Translation

Higher virtue is not conscious of virtue, therefore it has virtue;

Lower virtue never loses sight of virtue, therefore it has no virtue.

Higher virtue acts without intention;

Lower virtue acts with intention.

Higher benevolence acts without intention;

Higher righteousness acts with intention.

Higher ritual acts but when no one responds, it rolls up its sleeves and uses force.

Therefore, when the Tao is lost, virtue appears; when virtue is lost, benevolence appears; when benevolence is lost, righteousness appears; when righteousness is lost, ritual appears.

Ritual is the thinning of loyalty and trust, and the beginning of disorder.

Commentary

This chapter begins the second part of the Tao Te Ching and presents a hierarchy of moral development. The highest virtue is unconscious and natural, while lower forms become increasingly self-aware and forced. Laozi traces the decline from the Tao to virtue, then to benevolence, righteousness, and finally ritual—each step representing a further departure from natural harmony. Ritual, though necessary in a degraded society, is seen as the beginning of social disorder because it replaces genuine feeling with empty form.
