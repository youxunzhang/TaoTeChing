Chapter 50

Life and Death

Original Chinese

出生入死。
生之徒，十有三；
死之徒，十有三；
人之生，动之于死地，
亦十有三。
夫何故？以其生之厚。
善摄生者，
陆行不遇兕虎，
入军不被甲兵。
兕无所投其角，
虎无所用其爪，
兵无所容其刃。
夫何故？以其无死地。

English Translation

Out of life, death enters.
The followers of life are three in ten;
The followers of death are three in ten;
And men just passing from life to death
also number three in ten.
Why is this so?
Because they live their lives on the gross level.
He who is good at preserving his life
does not fear rhinoceros or tiger on land,
nor is he hurt by weapons in battle.
The rhinoceros finds no place to thrust its horn,
The tiger finds no place to use its claws,
The weapon finds no place to drive its blade.
Why is this so?
Because he has no vulnerable spot.

Transcending Mortality

This profound chapter explores the relationship between life and death, suggesting that those who truly understand life transcend the ordinary vulnerabilities that lead to destruction. By living in harmony with the Tao and avoiding excessive attachment to material existence, one becomes invulnerable to the forces that harm others. This isn't about physical immortality, but about spiritual protection that comes from right living.
