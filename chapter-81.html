Chapter 81 - Final Chapter

The True Words

Original Chinese

信言不美，美言不信。
善者不辩，辩者不善。
知者不博，博者不知。
圣人不积，
既以为人己愈有，
既以与人己愈多。
天之道，利而不害；
圣人之道，为而不争。

English Translation

True words are not beautiful;
Beautiful words are not true.
Those who are good do not argue;
Those who argue are not good.
Those who know are not learned;
The learned do not know.
The sage does not accumulate anything,
but gives everything to others and has even more;
he gives to others and his abundance increases.
The way of heaven is to benefit others without harming;
The way of the sage is to act without competing.

Final Reflection

The final chapter of the Tao Te Ching presents the ultimate wisdom: the distinction between appearance and reality, between superficial knowledge and true understanding. It reveals that the sage's way is one of giving rather than accumulating, of helping rather than competing.

This concluding chapter encapsulates the entire philosophy of the Tao Te Ching—that true power comes from humility, true wealth from generosity, and true wisdom from simplicity. The way of heaven and the way of the sage both benefit all beings without harm or conflict.
