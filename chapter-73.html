Chapter 73

The Courage of Daring

Original Chinese

勇於敢則殺，勇於不敢則活。
此兩者，或利或害。
天之所惡，孰知其故？
是以聖人猶難之。
天之道，不爭而善勝，
不言而善應，
不召而自來，
繟然而善謀。
天網恢恢，疏而不失。

English Translation

The courage of daring leads to death;
the courage of not daring leads to life.
Of these two, one brings benefit, the other harm.
What Heaven dislikes, who can know the reason?
Even the sage finds this difficult.
The Way of Heaven
does not contend, yet skillfully conquers;
does not speak, yet skillfully responds;
does not summon, yet things come naturally;
seems relaxed, yet skillfully plans.
Heaven's net is vast and wide;
though its mesh is coarse, nothing escapes.

Heaven's Justice

This chapter contrasts two types of courage: aggressive daring versus cautious restraint. The Tao operates through natural consequences rather than forceful intervention. Heaven's net (天網) represents cosmic justice - though it may seem loose and unnoticeable, ultimately nothing escapes the natural order of consequences.
