Chapter 65

The Ancient

Original Chinese

古之善為道者，非以明民，將以愚之。
民之難治，以其智多。
故以智治國，國之賊；
不以智治國，國之福。
知此兩者亦稽式。
常知稽式，是謂「玄德」。
玄德深矣，遠矣，與物反矣，
然後乃至大順。

English Translation

In ancient times, those who were skilled in the Way
did not enlighten the people, but kept them ignorant.
The difficulty in governing people
arises from their knowing too much.
Therefore, to govern a state with cleverness
is to harm the state;
to govern a state without cleverness
is to benefit the state.
To know these two principles is to know the pattern.
Always knowing this pattern is called Mysterious Virtue.
Mysterious Virtue is profound and far-reaching;
it returns with all things
to the great natural order.

Mysterious Virtue

This chapter explores the paradox of knowledge and governance. The "ignorance" mentioned here refers not to stupidity, but to the natural simplicity and contentment that comes from not being overstimulated by excessive intellectual complexity. Mysterious Virtue (玄德) represents the deep, intuitive wisdom that operates beyond conventional cleverness.
