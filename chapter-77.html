Chapter 77

The Way of Heaven

Original Chinese

天之道，其猶張弓與？
高者抑之，下者舉之；
有餘者損之，不足者補之。
天之道，損有餘而補不足。
人之道，則不然，
損不足以奉有餘。
孰能有餘以奉天下，唯有道者。
是以聖人為而不恃，
功成而不處，
其不欲見賢。

English Translation

Is not the Way of Heaven like drawing a bow?
The high is lowered, the low is raised;
what has excess is reduced, what is lacking is supplemented.
The Way of Heaven reduces excess and supplements deficiency.
The way of humans is different:
it reduces the deficient to serve those who have excess.
Who can have excess and offer it to the world?
Only those who possess the Way.
Therefore the sage acts but does not rely on his actions,
accomplishes but does not dwell on accomplishments;
he does not want to display his worthiness.

Natural Balance

This chapter uses the metaphor of drawing a bow to illustrate Heaven's way of maintaining balance. Nature automatically adjusts extremes - raising the low and lowering the high. In contrast, human society often does the opposite, taking from the poor to give to the rich. The sage follows Heaven's way by sharing abundance without seeking recognition.
