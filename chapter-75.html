Chapter 75

The People

Original Chinese

民之飢，以其上食稅之多，是以飢。
民之難治，以其上之有為，是以難治。
民之輕死，以其上求生之厚，是以輕死。
夫唯無以生為者，是賢於貴生。

English Translation

People starve
because their rulers consume too much in taxes;
therefore they starve.
People are difficult to govern
because their rulers are too active;
therefore they are difficult to govern.
People take death lightly
because their rulers demand too much for living;
therefore they take death lightly.
Those who do not act for the sake of living
are wiser than those who value life too highly.

The Cost of Excessive Government

This chapter critiques excessive taxation and governmental interference. When rulers take too much from the people and are overly active in governing, they create the very problems they seek to solve. The paradox is that those who are less attached to life often live more wisely than those who desperately cling to it.
