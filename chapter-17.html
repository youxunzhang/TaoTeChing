Chapter 17

The Highest Rulers

Original Chinese

太上，下知有之；
其次，親而譽之；
其次，畏之；
其次，侮之。
信不足焉，有不信焉。
悠兮，其貴言。
功成事遂，百姓皆謂：「我自然。」

English Translation

The best ruler is one whose existence is barely known.
Next comes one who is loved and praised.
Next comes one who is feared.
Next comes one who is despised.
If you don't trust the people, you make them untrustworthy.
The great rulers are relaxed and sparse with their words.
When their work is done, the people say, "We did it ourselves."
