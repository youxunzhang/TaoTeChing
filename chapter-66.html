Chapter 66

The Rivers and Seas

Original Chinese

江海之所以能為百谷王者，
以其善下之，故能為百谷王。
是以聖人欲上民，必以言下之；
欲先民，必以身後之。
是以聖人處上而民不重，
處前而民不害。
是以天下樂推而不厭。
以其不爭，故天下莫能與之爭。

English Translation

Rivers and seas are able to be kings
of a hundred valleys
because they are good at staying low;
thus they can be kings of a hundred valleys.
Therefore, if sages want to be above people,
they must speak humbly to them;
if they want to lead people,
they must place themselves behind them.
Thus when sages are in high positions,
people do not feel oppressed;
when they are in front,
people do not feel harmed.
Therefore the world delights in praising them
and does not tire of them.
Because they do not contend,
no one in the world can contend with them.

Leadership Through Humility

This chapter uses the metaphor of rivers and seas to illustrate true leadership. Just as water naturally flows to the lowest places and becomes the master of all valleys, wise leaders achieve greatness through humility and service. By placing themselves below and behind others, they paradoxically rise above them.
