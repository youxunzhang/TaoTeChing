Chapter 63

Acting Without Action

Original Chinese

為無為，事無事，味無味。
大小多少。
報怨以德。
圖難於其易，為大於其細；
天下難事，必作於易，
天下大事，必作於細。
是以聖人終不為大，
故能成其大。

English Translation

Practice non-action.
Work without working.
Taste the tasteless.
Make the small great, the few many.
Respond to hatred with virtue.
Plan for the difficult while it is easy;
do the great while it is small.
The most difficult things in the world
must be done while they are still easy.
The greatest things in the world
must be done while they are still small.
Therefore the sage never has to struggle with the great;
thus he can achieve greatness.

Wu Wei - Effortless Action

This chapter presents the core Taoist concept of wu wei (無為) - often translated as "non-action" but better understood as "effortless action." It's not about doing nothing, but about acting in harmony with the natural flow of things. The sage accomplishes great things by dealing with problems when they are small and easy to handle, never forcing or struggling against the natural order.
