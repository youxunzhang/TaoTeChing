Chapter 74

The Great Executioner

Original Chinese

民不畏死，奈何以死懼之？
若使民常畏死，而為奇者，
吾得執而殺之，孰敢？
常有司殺者殺。
夫代司殺者殺，
是謂代大匠斫，
夫代大匠斫者，
希有不傷其手矣。

English Translation

If people do not fear death,
how can you threaten them with death?
If people are made to constantly fear death
and someone acts rebelliously,
I can seize and execute them;
who would dare?
There is always an official executioner who kills.
To take the place of the official executioner and kill
is like taking the place of a master carpenter and cutting.
Those who take the place of a master carpenter
rarely avoid cutting their own hands.

Natural Justice

This chapter warns against usurping the natural role of consequences. Just as there is a natural order for life and death, there is a proper role for justice. When people try to take on the role of the "great executioner" (natural law), they often harm themselves in the process, like an amateur attempting the work of a master craftsman.
