Chapter 34

Chinese Original

大道泛兮，其可左右。

万物恃之以生而不辞，功成而不有。

衣养万物而不为主，可名于小；

万物归焉而不为主，可名于大。

以其终不自为大，故能成其大。

English Translation

The great Tao flows everywhere, to the left and to the right.

All things depend on it for life, and it does not refuse them; it accomplishes its work but does not claim credit.

It clothes and feeds all things but does not act as their master—it can be called small.

All things return to it, yet it does not act as their master—it can be called great.

Because it never considers itself great, it achieves true greatness.

Commentary

This chapter reveals the paradoxical nature of the Tao's greatness through its humility. The Tao flows everywhere without discrimination, nurtures all things without claiming ownership, and achieves greatness precisely because it never seeks to be great. This teaches us that true leadership and influence come not from asserting dominance but from serving others without seeking recognition or control.
