Chapter 67

Three Treasures

Original Chinese

天下皆謂我道大，似不肖。
夫唯大，故似不肖。
若肖，久矣其細也夫！
我有三寶，持而保之。
一曰慈，二曰儉，三曰不敢為天下先。
慈故能勇；儉故能廣；
不敢為天下先，故能成器長。
今舍慈且勇；舍儉且廣；
舍後且先；死矣！
夫慈以戰則勝，以守則固。
天將救之，以慈衛之。

English Translation

All the world says that my Way is great
but appears worthless.
It is great, therefore it appears worthless.
If it appeared worthy, it would have been proven small long ago!
I have three treasures
which I hold and preserve:
The first is compassion;
the second is frugality;
the third is not daring to be first in the world.
From compassion comes courage;
from frugality comes generosity;
from not daring to be first
comes the ability to lead.
Today people abandon compassion but try to be courageous;
abandon frugality but try to be generous;
abandon following but try to lead.
This is death!
Compassion in battle brings victory;
compassion in defense brings strength.
When Heaven is about to save someone,
it protects them with compassion.

The Three Treasures

This is one of the most important chapters in the Tao Te Ching, outlining the three fundamental virtues: compassion (慈), frugality (儉), and humility (不敢為天下先). These treasures work paradoxically - true courage comes from compassion, true generosity from frugality, and true leadership from following.
